Dissertation Research

Chinese Ship Models as Cultural Translation

My doctoral research focuses on the intersection of museum collections of early 20th century Chinese seagoing ship-models. How ship models as carriers of Cultural Translation and Western Perspectives in Scientific Records.

Through advanced 3D documentation techniques, I am creating a comprehensive database of Chinese ship models housed in Western institutions (US, Belgium, France), analyzing their provenance and historical significance. By comparing these models with historical records and archaeological evidence, I aim to advance understanding of shipwreck structures and Chinese shipbuilding traditions, as well as Western nautical study traditions, bridging not just different regions but also museum collections with archaeological research.

Research Impact & Applications

Bridging Museums and Archaeology

My research contributes to bridging the gap between museum collections and archaeological fieldwork, creating new methodologies for understanding maritime cultural heritage. By applying digital documentation techniques to museum ship models and comparing them with underwater archaeological evidence, I am developing innovative approaches to maritime archaeological interpretation.

Key Contributions
Digital Heritage Innovation: Pioneering the use of 3D documentation to create accessible digital archives of Chinese ship models in Western museums
Cross-Cultural Analysis: Developing frameworks for understanding how cultural knowledge is translated and preserved in museum settings
Public Accessibility: Creating resources that make maritime heritage research accessible to both academic and public audiences
Community Partnership: Establishing collaborative research relationships between international institutions and local communities

Future Research Directions

Looking ahead, my research will continue to explore the intersection of digital heritage, maritime archaeology, and cultural translation. Future projects include expanding the digital archive of Chinese ship models, developing virtual reality applications for public engagement, and investigating other forms of maritime cultural exchange between East and West.

I am particularly interested in how emerging technologies can be used to make maritime heritage more accessible while respecting cultural sensitivities and community ownership of heritage narratives. This includes developing collaborative research methodologies that prioritize community voices and local knowledge systems.
