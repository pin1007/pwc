Science Communication Philosophy

Archaeology originates from the exploration of the past. I believe that as a branch of anthropology, archaeology encompasses all aspects of human study. Therefore, archaeologists face the fascinating yet challenging task of interpreting the diverse array of artifacts left behind by past human societies within the span of a single lifetime. This makes the discipline both captivating and complex.

Through science communication, I hope to inspire people's curiosity about archaeology beyond popular misconceptions about aliens or Atlantis, helping them understand genuine archaeological research—the scientific methods, humanistic care, and continuous thinking and questioning that define our field. Having received training in journalism, I understand that media can have both positive and negative impacts. I aim to harness the power of diverse storytelling through text, video, and visual narratives to make underwater cultural heritage and underwater archaeology more accessible to broader audiences.
